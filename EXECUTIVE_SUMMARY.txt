## MAIN INSIGHTS

1. **Geographic Concentration**: Crime is concentrated in specific regions. The top 5 states account for 24% of all crimes despite being just 7% of states.

2. **Domestic Violence Crisis**: Domestic violence is by far the most common crime type, representing nearly 40% of all cases. This is a systemic issue requiring dedicated resources.

3. **Growing Trend**: The consistent increase over 20 years shows this is not a temporary issue but a sustained and growing problem.

4. **Regional Variation**: Different states face different crime profiles - suggesting different root causes and requiring different intervention strategies.

5. **Data-Driven Approach**: The clustering analysis provides a scientific basis for grouping states and allocating resources efficiently.
